Zomato's Strategic Evolution

Unpacking the Business Requirements for Sustainable Growth, Enhanced Market Dominance, and Future Innovation.

Project Overview

The Zomato Business Requirement Document outlines the comprehensive needs for the continued evolution and strategic expansion of Zomato's integrated food-tech ecosystem, encompassing its core food delivery, dining-out services, quick commerce (Blinkit), and B2B restaurant supplies (Hyperpure). This document serves as a foundational guide for all stakeholders, detailing the functional and non-functional requirements necessary to achieve Zomato's overarching business goals of sustainable profitability, enhanced market dominance, and the integration of advanced technologies and sustainability initiatives across its diverse service offerings.

Business Goals & Objectives

Overall Strategic Goals

The overarching objective is to achieve meaningful scale and sustained profits across all key business segments. This includes a deliberate shift from a growth-at-all-costs strategy to prioritizing profitability, as evidenced by the company reporting a profit of INR 351 crore in FY24, a significant turnaround from a loss of INR 917 crore in FY23. The company aims to improve unit economics through dynamic pricing models and cost optimization strategies to enhance profit margins per transaction.

Segment-Specific Goals

  • Food Delivery: Maintain stability and ensure continued growth, leveraging the large runway for expansion in India.
  • Quick Commerce (Blinkit): Achieve aggressive expansion, aiming for 2,000 stores by December 2025, with visibility for further expansion to 3,000 stores, to significantly increase its addressable market and cross-leverage the existing customer base and delivery fleet.
  • B2B Supplies (Hyperpure): Drive strong adoption and growth, with the vision for this business to become as large as or even larger than the food delivery business due to its larger addressable market.
  • Going-out (Dining-out & Ticketing): Scale to $3 billion in annual topline (Net Order Value) with $150 million of Adjusted EBITDA over the next five years, focusing on premium customer base and diverse use cases.

Stakeholders & Their Needs

Zomato Leadership & Executives

Concerned with overall strategic direction, financial performance (profitability, revenue growth), market share, long-term sustainability, and investor relations.

Product Management & Engineering Leads

Responsible for defining product roadmaps, translating business requirements into technical specifications, managing development teams, and ensuring technical feasibility and quality.

Sales & Marketing Teams

Focused on customer acquisition, retention, brand building, promotional strategies, and revenue generation. They require features that enable effective campaigns and provide insights into market performance.

Operations & Logistics Teams

Concerned with the efficiency, scalability, and reliability of delivery operations, restaurant onboarding, and delivery partner management. They need tools for real-time monitoring, issue resolution, and optimization.

Finance & Legal Teams

Ensure financial compliance, accurate revenue reporting, secure payment processing, commission structures, and adherence to legal and regulatory frameworks.

Investors & Shareholders

Interested in Zomato's financial health, growth trajectory, profitability, and return on investment.

Regulatory Bodies

Oversee compliance with industry-specific regulations (e.g., food safety, consumer protection) and broader legal frameworks.

Scope of the System

The Zomato platform encompasses a broad range of services designed to create a comprehensive food-tech ecosystem. The primary services and functionalities within the scope of the Zomato system include:

  • Food Ordering and Delivery: Core functionality enabling users to browse restaurants, select menu items, place orders, and receive doorstep delivery.
  • Dining Out Services: Features for restaurant discovery, detailed menu viewing, ratings, reviews, and table reservations. This also includes event-based monetization through initiatives like Zomaland.
  • Quick Commerce (Blinkit): Rapid delivery of groceries and other merchandise from dark stores, typically under 15 minutes.
  • B2B Supplies (Hyperpure): Supply of fresh, hygienic, and high-quality food ingredients and kitchen supplies to restaurants and other B2B buyers.
  • Intercity Delivery (Zomato Legends/Quick): Premium service for ordering iconic food items from different cities for delivery across India.
  • Restaurant SaaS Tools: Premium software services for restaurant partners, including CRM dashboards, order analytics, inventory management, and ad campaign management.
  • White Label Access: Services enabling restaurants to develop customized food delivery applications and consultancy for cloud kitchens/expansion.
  • Zomato Kitchens: Partnerships for establishing and managing kitchen infrastructure services for selected restaurant operators.

Business Requirements: User Stories

User Registration & Discovery:

  • As a new customer, I want to easily register and log in using multiple options (e.g., email, social media), so that I can quickly access Zomato's services.
  • As a customer, I want to search for restaurants by cuisine, location, or dish, and filter results by preferences (e.g., dietary, price), so that I can efficiently find exactly what I'm looking for.
  • As a customer, I want to receive AI-powered restaurant and dish recommendations, so that I can discover new options tailored to my tastes.

Ordering & Payment:

  • As a customer, I want to add items to my cart and customize my order (e.g., add notes, modify ingredients), so that I get my meal exactly as I want it.
  • As a customer, I want to have multiple secure payment options (e.g., UPI, cards, wallets, COD), so that I can complete my order conveniently and safely.

Post-Order Experience:

  • As a customer, I want to track my order in real-time on a map, including the delivery partner's location, so that I know exactly when my food will arrive.
  • As a customer, I want to provide ratings and reviews for restaurants and delivery experiences, so that I can share my feedback and help others make informed choices.

Onboarding & Profile Management:

  • As a restaurant owner, I want to easily onboard my restaurant and manage my profile details (e.g., timings, menu, FSSAI info), so that my business is accurately represented on the platform.
  • As a restaurant owner, I want to manage my menu items, including adding, editing, and marking items in/out of stock, so that my online menu is always up-to-date.

Order & Business Management:

  • As a restaurant staff member, I want to receive instant notifications for new orders and efficiently accept/process them, so that I can fulfill customer orders promptly.
  • As a restaurant owner, I want to access comprehensive business reports (e.g., sales, payouts, customer funnel), so that I can analyze my performance and make data-driven decisions.
  • As a restaurant owner, I want to create and manage promotional offers and advertisements, so that I can attract more customers and boost sales.

Availability & Order Flow:

  • As a delivery partner, I want to easily log in and manage my availability, so that I can receive delivery assignments when I'm ready to work.
  • As a delivery partner, I want to receive clear notifications for new orders and accept/decline them efficiently, so that I can manage my delivery tasks effectively.

Navigation & Support:

  • As a delivery partner, I want route optimization tools within the app, so that I can find the most efficient path to pick up and deliver orders.
  • As a delivery partner, I want to track my earnings transparently within the app, so that I can monitor my income.
  • As a delivery partner, I want in-app chat support and access to emergency services, so that I can quickly resolve issues or get help when needed.

User & Content Management:

  • As an administrator, I want to manage customer, restaurant, and delivery partner accounts (e.g., activate, suspend, update details), so that I can maintain platform integrity and provide support.
  • As an administrator, I want to manage restaurant listings, menus, and promotional content across the platform, so that I can ensure accuracy and relevance.

Monitoring & Reporting:

  • As an operations manager, I want to monitor all live orders and intervene in case of issues, so that I can ensure smooth operations and resolve problems quickly.
  • As a finance analyst, I want to generate detailed reports on revenue, commissions, and payouts across all business segments, so that I can track financial performance and ensure accurate settlements.

Requirement Metrics (KPIs)

  • Average Monthly Transacting Customers (AMTC): Indicates the size and activity of the core customer base.
  • Order Frequency (per customer): Reflects customer engagement and stickiness.
  • Customer Retention Rate: Measures the effectiveness of loyalty programs and overall customer satisfaction.
  • Net Promoter Score (NPS): Gauges overall customer satisfaction and loyalty.
  • Average Order Value (AOV): Indicates customer spending habits and potential for revenue growth per transaction.
  • Average Delivery Time: Directly impacts customer satisfaction and quick commerce competitiveness.
  • Order Fulfillment Rate: Measures reliability and operational accuracy.
  • Restaurant Partner Onboarding Time: Reflects efficiency of partner acquisition processes.
  • Delivery Partner Utilization Rate: Measures efficiency of fleet management and earning opportunities for partners.
  • Customer Support Resolution Time: Reflects responsiveness and quality of support.
  • Gross Order Value (GOV): Primary indicator of overall business scale and transaction volume.
  • Adjusted Revenue: Provides a more accurate picture of core earnings.
  • Adjusted EBITDA: Key indicator of the company's ability to generate profit from its operations.
  • Take Rate (Commission Rate): Measures the profitability of each transaction.
  • Blinkit Store Count: Direct measure of quick commerce expansion progress.
  • Hyperpure Revenue Growth: Measures the scaling of the B2B business.
  • Percentage of EV-based Deliveries: Measures progress towards net-zero emissions.
  • Plastic Neutrality Achievement: Reflects commitment to environmental responsibility.
  • Number of PwD (Persons with Disabilities) Delivery Partners: Measures progress in inclusive hiring and social impact.
  • Number of Women in Logistics Roles: Measures gender diversity in the workforce.
